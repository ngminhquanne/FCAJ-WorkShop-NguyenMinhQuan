* Update the latest technology trends and share practical experience in Cloud and AI.
* Create networking and connection opportunities among engineers, tech experts, and participating students.
* Discuss practical problems, infrastructure optimization solutions, and career development directions in the IT industry.

### Key Highlights

#### Level Up Your AWS Skill with Cloud Quest and Floci

* **Get familiar with Cloud Quest:** Experience gamified learning methods to practice practical AWS skills.
* **Build infrastructure knowledge:** Explore how to apply Floci to design and deploy cloud resources.
* **Practical application:** Summarize experiences from simulated labs to apply to real-world cloud projects.

#### HACKATHON

* **Spirit of innovation:** Challenge yourself in developing technology products within a limited timeframe.
* **Idea realization process:** From defining problems and brainstorming system design concepts to deploying complete solutions.
* **Teamwork skills:** Coordinate effectively under time pressure and optimize solution presentations before the board of judges.

#### Why We Always Need Confidence

* **The importance of confidence:** Overcome psychological barriers when approaching new and complex technologies.
* **Proactively facing challenges:** How to maintain a steadfast mindset when operating or handling incidents within projects.
* **Self-development:** Build strong professional courage within the information technology industry.

#### Tu Vi Dai Viet

* **Product research and development:** The journey of building a practical application from data analysis to system completion.
* **Optimizing user experience:** Combine intuitive interfaces with smooth backend processing capabilities.
* **Practical deployment lessons:** Experiences learned during architectural design and launching applications to users.

* **Perspective on the "hidden iceberg":** Identify hidden risks in software projects lacking standardized management processes.
* **The importance of DevOps:** The vital role of automation, continuous testing, and monitoring system setup.
* **Disaster prevention:** Build backup strategies and secure CI/CD processes to minimize production system failures.

### What Was Learned

#### Design Thinking

* **Business-first approach:** Always start from the business domain, not technology
* **Ubiquitous language:** Importance of a common vocabulary between business and tech teams
* **Bounded contexts:** How to identify and manage complexity in large systems

#### Technical Architecture

* **Event storming technique:** Practical method to model business processes
* **Use Event-driven communication** instead of synchronous calls
* **Integration patterns:** Understand when to use sync, async, pub/sub, streaming
* **Compute spectrum:** Selection criteria from VM $\rightarrow$ containers $\rightarrow$ serverless

#### Modernization Strategy

* **Phased approach:** Do not rush; must have a clear roadmap
* **7Rs framework:** Different pathways depending on the characteristics of each application
* **ROI measurement:** Cost reduction + business agility

#### Work Application

* **Apply DDD to current project:** Event storming sessions with the business team
* **Refactor microservices:** Use bounded contexts to identify service boundaries
* **Implement event-driven patterns:** Replace some sync calls with async messaging
* **Serverless adoption:** Pilot AWS Lambda for suitable use cases
* **Try Amazon Q Developer:** Integrate into the development workflow to boost productivity

### Event Experience

Participating in the workshop "GenAI-powered App-DB Modernization" was a very rewarding experience, giving me a comprehensive view of how to modernize applications and databases using modern methods and tools. Some highlights include:

#### Learning from Expert Speakers

* Speakers from AWS and major technology organizations shared **best practices** in modern application design.
* Through practical case studies, I gained a clearer understanding of how to apply **Domain-Driven Design (DDD)** and **Event-Driven Architecture** to large projects.

#### Practical Technical Experience

* Participating in presentations on **event storming** helped me visualize how to **model business processes** into domain events.
* Learned how to **analyze microservices** and define **bounded contexts** to manage the complexity of large systems.
* Understood the **trade-offs** between **synchronous** and **asynchronous communication** as well as integration patterns like **pub/sub, point-to-point, streaming**.

#### Application of Modern Tools

* Directly explored **Amazon Q Developer**, an AI tool supporting the SDLC from planning to maintenance.
* Learned how to **automate code transformation** and pilot serverless with **AWS Lambda**, thereby enhancing development productivity.

#### Connection and Exchange

* The workshop provided opportunities to directly exchange ideas with experts, colleagues, and the business team, helping to **enhance the ubiquitous language** between business and tech.
* Through practical examples, I realized the importance of the **business-first approach**, always starting from business needs rather than focusing solely on technology.

#### Key Takeaways

* Applying DDD and event-driven patterns helps reduce **coupling**, increase **scalability**, and enhance **resilience** for the system.
* A modernization strategy requires a **phased approach** and **ROI** measurement; one should not rush into converting the entire system all at once.
* AI tools like Amazon Q Developer can **boost productivity** if integrated into the current development workflow.
